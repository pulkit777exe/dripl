# Product

## Register
product

## Users
Product teams and small teams who need to collaborate on diagrams, wireframes, and visual thinking. They work together in real-time, capturing ideas, shaping direction, and sharing decisions in one calm workspace. Context: teams collaborating during brainstorming sessions, design reviews, and planning meetings.

## Product Purpose
Dripl is a real-time collaborative whiteboard that helps product teams capture ideas, shape direction, and share decisions. Success means teams can quickly sketch, organize, and iterate on visual concepts together without friction. The product serves the workflow of collaborative visual thinking and decision-making.

## Brand Personality
Warm and approachable. The interface should feel friendly and inviting, not cold or corporate. Think of it as a calm workspace where teams can focus on ideas together. Three words: warm, approachable, collaborative.

## Anti-references
SaaS clichés and generic patterns. Avoid:
- Generic SaaS templates with purple gradients
- Massive icons everywhere
- Hero-metric templates (big number, small label, gradient accent)
- Identical card grids repeated endlessly
- Modal abuse (modals as first thought)
- Side-stripe borders as colored accents
- Glassmorphism used decoratively
- Gradient text for emphasis

## Design Principles
1. **Warmth over coldness** - Use warm cream tones and approachable interactions, create a feeling of calm workspace
2. **Clarity over complexity** - Keep the canvas and tools intuitive, not overwhelming; prioritize ease of use
3. **Collaboration first** - Design for real-time teamwork, not just individual use; make presence and sharing natural
4. **Calm focus** - Create a workspace that feels peaceful, not chaotic; reduce visual noise
5. **Hand-drawn humanity** - Embrace the sketch-like aesthetic as a feature, not a limitation; celebrate the imperfect

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Basic accessibility with keyboard navigation and color contrast. Support screen readers where practical, but prioritize visual design and interaction quality. Consider reduced motion preferences and ensure color contrast meets WCAG AA standards where feasible.
